Each service should have: a short description, a placeholder image, estimated timeline range (e.g., "2-5 days"), a price range or "Contact for quote", and a CTA that routes to the booking form.
For booking — go with a "Request a Consultation" form, not a full scheduling calendar. Most performance shops don't do same-day bookings. The flow is: customer fills out a form, the shop reviews it and reaches out to schedule. The form should capture: name, phone, email, vehicle year/make/model, service(s) interested in (multi-select from the list above), brief description of what they're looking for, preferred contact method (call vs. email), and an optional file upload field for photos of their current setup.
For handling submissions — just email notification for now. Form submits, shop gets an email with the details. Scaffold it so a CRM or more advanced notification system could plug in later, but don't build any actual CRM integration. Keep the backend simple — the form data hits an API route that could be wired to any email service or webhook down the road.
