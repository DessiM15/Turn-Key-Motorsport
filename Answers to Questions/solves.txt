What Turnkey Motorsports is solving:
They're a physical performance shop that builds engines, sells parts, and does installations across all makes and models. Right now, their reach is limited to whoever drives past the shop or hears about them through word of mouth. The problem is that their competitors — shops like Calvo, FRP, Underground, Fathouse — already have strong online presences that let them sell parts nationally, showcase their builds, and establish credibility before a customer ever picks up the phone. Without a site, Turnkey is invisible to the vast majority of their potential market.
What the site needs to do:
At its core, this site needs to do three things simultaneously. First, it needs to sell parts and packages online — turning Turnkey from a local shop into a national (or at least regional) e-commerce business. The tiered engine packages, the parts catalog, the cart and checkout flow — that's the revenue engine. Second, it needs to generate service leads — people who find the site, see the builds gallery, read the testimonials, and book an appointment to bring their car in. The gallery and testimonials pages aren't just nice-to-haves, they're the trust infrastructure that converts a browser into a customer. Third, it needs to establish authority and credibility in a space where trust is everything. Car enthusiasts are spending thousands of dollars on engine work. They're not handing their keys to someone with no online presence. The blog, the FAQ, the detailed package breakdowns, the past builds with HP numbers — all of that exists to answer the unspoken question: "Can I trust these guys with my car?"
Why it needs to exist:
Because in the performance automotive world, your website is your reputation. Enthusiasts research online before they spend. They compare shops, they look at build galleries, they read reviews, they check what packages are offered and at what price points. A shop without a serious online presence doesn't just lose online sales — it loses walk-in customers too, because people Google you before they visit. The four reference sites your client picked aren't just design inspiration — they're the competitive benchmark. Turnkey needs to be at that level or above to be taken seriously in this space.
The site isn't just a storefront. It's proof that Turnkey Motorsports is legitimate, capable, and worth the investment.
